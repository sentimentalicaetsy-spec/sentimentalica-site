50 Things You Should Never Throw Away If You Junk Journal

July 6, 2026 · 2 minute read

Every junk journaler has the same origin story: one day you are about to throw something away, and a small voice says — wait. This is the list of fifty things that voice is talking about. Pin it above your bin.

Paper that already has a life

1. Envelopes with beautiful stamps · 2. handwritten letters, even boring ones · 3. receipts from meaningful days · 4. train and bus tickets · 5. museum and cinema stubs · 6. old book pages from damaged books · 7. dictionary and atlas pages · 8. sheet music · 9. yellowed newspaper columns · 10. instruction leaflets in lovely typefaces.

The point is not that these are rare — it is that they are yours. A tram ticket glued next to a painted page turns decoration into memory.

Packaging you paid for anyway

11. Tea box panels · 12. teabag envelopes (steam them open) · 13. chocolate wrappers with gold foil · 14. paper bags from bakeries · 15. tissue paper from parcels · 16. clothing tags · 17. string and twine from packages · 18. stamps steamed off parcels · 19. coffee sleeves · 20. the corrugated liner inside biscuit boxes.

Nature that dries beautifully

21. Flat flowers (pansies are champions) · 22. lavender sprigs · 23. small leaves in autumn colour · 24. ferns · 25. grasses and seed heads · 26. flower petals for scatter · 27. a four-leaf clover if you are lucky · 28. thin bark that has already fallen · 29. pressed herbs from cooking · 30. the first tiny leaf of spring.

Fabric, thread and soft things

31. Lace trim from worn-out clothes · 32. ribbon from gift wrapping · 33. buttons, always buttons · 34. denim scraps · 35. embroidery thread ends · 36. a square of a beloved old shirt · 37. netting from fruit bags · 38. zips from broken pouches · 39. felt offcuts · 40. the satin label from a coat.

The unexpected ten

41. Paint chips from the hardware store · 42. old keys that open nothing · 43. hotel key cards and luggage tags · 44. pressed pennies and foreign coins (trace or rub them) · 45. broken jewellery chains · 46. wax seals from fancy mail · 47. photo-booth strips · 48. expired stamps from your own passport story · 49. the paper band from a new deck of cards · 50. anything with your grandmother's handwriting on it. Especially that.
