A Vintage Dog Show in Your Junk Journal: Ideas for Dog Lovers

July 6, 2026 · 2 minute read

Some journals are about flowers, and some are about the small brown eyes that watch you from under the table while you glue things. If your heart belongs to a dog — this theme was painted for you.

Dog Days is built like a vintage kennel-club scrapbook: prize rosettes with pleated olive ribbons, portrait medallions of solemn spaniels and cheerful terriers, and papers aged to that soft archive cream. The dogs here are painted like family members — because that is what they are, and that is exactly why a spread made with them will mean something in ten years.

The palette: worn leashes and old ribbons

Six spreads to make for your dog

1. Award your champion. Cut out a rosette and give your own dog a prize — "Best Napper, Autumn Division", "Champion of Stolen Socks" — with the citation in your most official handwriting.

2. A page per year. One rosette, one photograph, one line about what this year of their life was like. It becomes a keepsake faster than you expect.

3. The kennel-club ledger. Use the aged papers as journaling spots: vet visits, favourite walks, the names they actually answer to.

Everything in these spreads comes from one kit — the pages you see through this whole article:

4. Ribbon tails as page markers. The long pleated ribbons, cut out and glued to the page edge, hang out of the journal like prize tags.

5. A memorial page, gently. For the dogs we have said goodbye to: one portrait, one ribbon, and all the room you need. This theme carries that weight with dignity.

6. Let the pages rest together. The rosettes are detailed, so give them calm neighbours — kraft paper, old book pages, a charcoal border. Here is how the printed pages look off the screen:

Start with one spread

Print two or three pages, pick your calmest cream base, and begin with the award — every dog deserves a prize, and yours has been waiting.
