Junk Journaling

How to start a junk journal when you don't know where to begin

May 24, 2026 · 6 minute read

The hardest page of any junk journal is the first one. It stares back, blank and intimidating, and suddenly the dozens of beautiful papers you've collected feel impossible to choose from. So you close the journal. And it sits.

If this is you — welcome. This guide is for the moment before the first cut.

There are no rules. Truly.

Junk journaling is a craft built on imperfection. The whole point is that scraps become beautiful when you put them next to each other. A torn page from an old book. A ticket stub. A piece of patterned paper with coffee stains. A pressed flower. None of them have to "match" — they only have to feel like yours.

"A junk journal is not a finished object. It is a habit of noticing beautiful, useless things."

What you actually need to start

Replace this section with your real beginner's checklist when you're ready to write. Some ideas to cover: a base book or signature, glue, scissors, a few digital paper packs to print, ephemera (real or printed), and a willingness to leave things crooked.
